Wedding Guide

Sangeet function

30 January 2017 4:30-8:30 PM

A celebration held before the main wedding ceremony for the bride and groom along with friends and relatives.

Nichayathartham

01 February 2017 6:30-9:00 PM

This is a formal engagement with the bride and groom. The priest reads out the details of the marriage to be conducted, a sort of confirmation in the presence of both extended families.

Oonjal ceremony

02 February 2017 7:30 AM

The bride and the groom sit on the swing. Friends and relatives display their talent in classical music, providing festivity to the atmosphere. The oonjal (swing) signifies the ups and downs of life.

Maangalya Dhaaranam

02 February 2017 9:00-9:45 AM

The most precious moment of one's life. The bride sits on her father's lap. The groom ties the Mangalya Sutra around the bride's neck with prayers for their wedding and long life.

Nalangu

02 February 2017 3:30-4:30 PM

The evening of the marriage day is the time to relax and play. The newly-wed wife calls her husband for play. Much to the merriment of one and all gathered, there follows a list of playful items, the bride fanning the groom, showing him a mirror, breaking papads over each other's head, rolling a coconut between them and so on.

Vratham

01 February 2017 10:30-12:00 AM

This is conducted to convey a message to the groom that he has to prepare himself to move from Brahmacharyam (bachelorhood) to Grahasthashramam (family life). Prayers are conducted to seek the blessings of the ancestors.

Kashi Yatra

02 February 2017 6:30 AM

The groom decides to give up worldly pleasures and prepares to go to Kasi to lead the life of an ascetic. The parents and the Guru advice the groom to marry the bride to enter Grahasthashramam (family life). This is a ritual enacted prior to the actual wedding where the groom's uncle persuades him that he should get married and not go to Kasi.

Pani Grahanam

02 February 2017 8:30-9:00 AM

The groom holds the bride's right hand and recites the marriage vows in four mantras. He prays to Agni, the God of Fire, Saraswathi, the Goddess of knowledge; and Vayu, the Lord of Air for blessings, long life and confluence of mind.

Saptha padhi

02 February 2017 9:45-10:15 AM

Together, the couple takes seven steps around the sacred fire. Each step is an oath the husband and wife make to each other, and is the most important part of the ceremony.

Reception

02 February 2017 6:30-9:30 PM

A wedding reception is a party held after the completion of a marriage ceremony. It is held as hospitality for those who have attended the wedding, hence the name reception: the couple receives society, in the form of family and friends, for the first time as a married couple.
